Question 1

?

The following equation relates the numbers $r$ and $s ,$ where $s \neq 0$ and $r < 9 :$  
$$
r = 9 - \frac{27}{s} .
$$
 Which equation expresses $s$ in terms of $r ?$

| a | $s = \frac{r + 9}{27}$ |
| --- | --- |
| b | $s = \frac{r - 9}{27}$ |
| c | $s = - \frac{27}{r - 9}$ |
| d | $s = \frac{27}{r - 9}$ |
| e | $s = \frac{9 r - 27}{27}$ |

EXPLANATION

We are given the equation 
$$
r = 9 - \frac{27}{s}
$$
 and asked to solve for $s$ in terms of $r .$

First, we subtract $9$ from *both sides*: 
$$
\begin{aligned}r - 9 & = 9 - \frac{27}{s} - 9 \\ r - 9 & = - \frac{27}{s}\end{aligned}
$$

Next, we multiply *both sides* by $- 1 :$ 
$$
\begin{aligned}- \left(r - 9\right) & = \frac{27}{s}\end{aligned}
$$

Then multiply *both sides* by $s :$ 
$$
\begin{aligned}- \left(r - 9\right) s & = 27\end{aligned}
$$

Finally, divide *both sides* by $- \left(r - 9\right)$ to isolate $s :$ 
$$
\begin{aligned}\frac{- \left(r - 9\right) s}{- \left(r - 9\right)} & = \frac{27}{- \left(r - 9\right)} \\ s & = - \frac{27}{r - 9}\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore, the correct answer is 
$$
s = - \frac{27}{r - 9} .
$$

Question 2

?

The variables $m$ and $n$ are related by the equation  
$$
m = \frac{5 n + 6}{n} .
$$
 Which equation expresses $n$ in terms of $m ?$

| a | $n = \frac{5}{m - 6}$ |
| --- | --- |
| b | $n = \frac{6}{m - 5}$ |
| c | $n = \frac{1}{m - 6}$ |
| d | $n = \frac{5 n}{m - 6}$ |
| e | $n = \frac{5 m}{n + 6}$ |

EXPLANATION

Multiplying both sides of the given equation by $n$ gives 
$$
m n = 5 n + 6.
$$
 Subtracting $5 n$ from both sides, we get 
$$
m n - 5 n = 6.
$$
 Factoring out $n$ on the left-hand side, we get 
$$
n \left(m - 5\right) = 6.
$$
 Finally, dividing both sides by $m - 5$ yields 
$$
n = \frac{6}{m - 5} .
$$

So, the correct answer is 
$$
n = \frac{6}{m - 5} .
$$

Question 3

?

The variables $z$ and $q$ are related by the equation  
$$
z = \frac{6 q + 12}{q} .
$$
 Which equation expresses $q$ in terms of $z ?$

| a | $q = \frac{6 z}{q - 12}$ |
| --- | --- |
| b | $q = \frac{6}{z + 12}$ |
| c | $q = \frac{6 q}{z + 12}$ |
| d | $q = \frac{1}{z - 6}$ |
| e | $q = \frac{12}{z - 6}$ |

EXPLANATION

Multiplying both sides of the given equation by $q$ gives 
$$
z q = 6 q + 12.
$$
 Subtracting $6 q$ from both sides, we get 
$$
z q - 6 q = 12.
$$
 Factoring out $q$ on the left-hand side, we get 
$$
q \left(z - 6\right) = 12.
$$
 Finally, dividing both sides by $z - 6$ yields 
$$
q = \frac{12}{z - 6} .
$$

So, the correct answer is 
$$
q = \frac{12}{z - 6} .
$$
